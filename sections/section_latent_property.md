## The subspace as a latent property of the model

A hypothesis for the next experiment. The time-lagged probes are fitted on data, which means the specific directions they recover are by construction distribution-dependent. But that raises a question the current experiment cannot answer: is the persistent subspace a property of the model's weights that different distributions merely surface in different ways, or does the geometry itself change across distributions?

These are separable claims. The specific directions the probes identify will almost certainly shift when the distribution shifts; a subspace fitted on code is unlikely to point in the same directions as one fitted on C4. But the geometry underneath, the rank, the dimensionality, the internal structure, may be more stable. That stability, if it exists, would suggest the model has a latent capacity for persistent state baked into its weights, and that different data distributions activate different corners of it rather than producing fundamentally different structures.

The C4 result is actually mild evidence for this. C4 is already an extremely wide distribution, spanning topics, registers, domains, and lengths. The fact that a compact ~31-direction subspace emerged from that heterogeneous corpus rather than a diffuse high-dimensional cloud suggests the geometry is not just a statistical artifact of a narrow distribution. Something in the model's weights is organizing the persistent signal into a low-dimensional structure, regardless of what the tokens are about.

The right test is cross-distribution subspace overlap rather than direction identity. Fit time-lagged probes separately on two very different distributions, then measure the principal angles between the resulting subspaces. If the subspace rank and overlap are stable across distributions while the specific directions shift, that would support the hypothesis that the geometry is model-weight-dependent and the directions are distribution-dependent.
